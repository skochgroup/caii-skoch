SALUTING PROFESSIONALS

Professionals from corporate sector who rise from the ranks and build India while building a career, making an honest living without becoming business Tycoons or political leaders, have brought about The New India.

India – the way it is today is taken for granted. Infrastructure – digital or physical both have taken several decades to come up.

The role of the state has reduced and contributions from the corporate sector have increased.

In the glamour and the glitz of celebrating businessmen and leaders, the role of professionals is forgotten. They do not make award lists or even national honours.

CEO’s Association for Inclusive India (CAII) and SKOCH Group present SKOCH CEO’s Choice Award. It is a vote of thanks – a token of gratitude from a nation to its professionals.

The award is decided through Peer Evaluation and Domain Expert Consultation.

CEO's Association for Inclusive India (CAII)

C P Gurnani

Managing Director & CEO
Tech Mahindra Ltd

C P Gurnani

Managing Director & CEO, Tech Mahindra Ltd

Digital India has been forty years in the making and we are fortunate to have a ringside view of C P Gurnani’s numerous contributions to it.

India could not afford mainframes and it was his work with NIC that made HCL mini-computers, the first scale deployed multi-user systems ubiquitous in the government projects at the time. Even today, he continues to lead Digital India’s growth story from the forefront with strategic projects on various smart cities, GSTN and so on.

Besides his pioneering work in HCL, Gurnani led Tech Mahindra’s transformation journey into becoming a global digital powerhouse. He is also credited with one of the biggest turnarounds of Indian corporate history – the acquisition and merger of Satyam – and his passion for nurturing a digital future-ready talent pool.

Gurnani is known as a people person, who has significantly contributed towards realising the Digital India dream. He has contributed to society through various initiatives such as reskilling, child education and helping underprivileged children.

Sameer Kochhar

Secretary General, CAII and
Chairman, SKOCH Group & SKOCH Foundation

Sameer Kochhar

Secretary General, CAII and Chairman, SKOCH Group & SKOCH Foundation

Mr Sameer Kochhar is a social scientist, leading reforms historian in India and an acclaimed author. His domain expertise on socio-economic issues has been used by the government and private sector alike for consultancy on public policy and strategy. He has published 16 books including Defeating Poverty, ModiNomics and The Untold Story of Indian Reforms. He is privileged to have his books released by Dr Manmohan Singh, Mr Narendra Modi, Mr Arun Jaitley and Mr P. Chidambaram, among several other luminaries. His mantra is growth, livelihoods and equity.

He is an industry veteran with a multifaceted career spanning over 37 years with extensive experience in using action research to bring felt-needs to policy and strategy. He is credited with several well-performing public policy interventions in areas of digital, financial and social inclusion. Considered fearless, he is often seen standing up to be counted on issues of national importance, asking difficult questions and offering seasoned advice. He has assessed first-hand a large number of projects in India and has also created one of the biggest independent knowledge repositories on what works/ This also serves as perhaps the only institutional memory of reforms in India.

He has been a member of several expert groups and committees constituted by the Ministry of Panchayati Raj, Government of India, advocating a case for a strengthening of local self-governance through use of technology; and, the Ministry of Communications and IT on the evaluation of National e-Governance Plan. He was on the advisory committee of College of Agricultural Banking of Reserve Bank of India for nearly 6 years. He has represented India in inter-governmental conferences.

Kulmeet Bawa

President, CAII and
President & Managing Director, SAP India

Kulmeet Bawa

President, CAII and President & Managing Director, SAP India

Kulmeet Bawa is the chief architect of SAP’s growth and innovation strategies for the Indian Subcontinent. He is responsible for delivering bold transformations and exceptional SAP experiences to customers across the ecosystem, as well as guiding businesses in India, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka to adopt a digital-first mindset.

Over his 25 years of cross functional experience, Kulmeet has orchestrated business revolution by providing proven business outcomes for organizations across various industries and portfolios. Kulmeet was previously the Chief Operating Officer for Resulticks where he led the growth strategy and execution of go-to-market for the organization.

While at Adobe as Managing Director for India and South Asia, Kulmeet was instrumental in accelerating the organization’s growth momentum by expanding the business footprint within the Enterprise, SMB and Consumer sectors. Prior to Adobe, he has held various leadership roles managing enterprise development and business processes for major tech companies such as Microsoft Corporation and Sun Microsystems.

With a relentless focus on driving customer experience Kulmeet believes in nurturing and mentoring team members, promoting design thinking, creativity and innovative culture. He also actively contributes towards social initiatives which focus on education and women empowerment.

Prior to his corporate avatar, Kulmeet Bawa spent 12 years with the Indian Armoured Corps as a Cavalry officer. During his tenure with the Indian army, Kulmeet was Aide de Camp to the Governor of an Indian State and taught Strategy at the prestigious Armoured Corps Centre and School.

Kulmeet is a graduate from the Indian School of Business, Hyderabad, and an alumnus of the prestigious National Defence Academy.

Author of the Year: The Maverick Effect

Harish Mehta

Founder & Executive Chairman
Onward Technologies

Harish Mehta

Founder & Executive Chairman, Onward Technologies

Mr. Harish Mehta is the author of the national bestseller ‘The Maverick Effect’ and the driving force behind galvanizing the IT industry in India by co-founding NASSCOM, the IT industry’s association. He was elected as the first Chairman of NASSCOM and continues to be the Convener of NASSCOM’s Chairman’s Council.

Harish Mehta has a long history of success as an Indian entrepreneur, investor, and changemaker. Mr. Mehta has founded, led, grown & mentored the Onward Group. From 1993 to 2005 he served as the Deputy Chairman and MD of Onward Novell Software India Private Limited, a JV with the world's 2nd largest personal software company at the time. Prior to founding Onward, he was one of the chief architects of the Hinditron–Digital (DEC) JV in India in 1987.

He introduced Silicon Valley’s, The Indus Entrepreneurs (TiE) to India and is the co-founder of the Indian Angel Network (IAN), one of the world's largest and most active angel investor groups. Harish Mehta is also a founding member of Infinity Venture Fund, India’s first corporate VC fund, and an active volunteer of Onward Foundation & Dewang Mehta Foundation.

Radhika Gupta

Going Beyond the Call of Duty

Ajay Kumar

Former Defence Secretary
Government of India

Ajay Kumar

Former Defence Secretary, Government of India

With ‘Make in India’ at his heart, Ajay Kumar has pushed both the electronics and the defence manufacturing industry in India to emerge as a leading player on the global map. During his time, the defence exports scaled up to Rs 13,000 cr for 2021-22. Undoubtedly, the credit for this goes squarely to his vision of introduction of modern warfare capabilities, disruptive technologies and innovations. He established the iDEX initiative to spur a cost-effective defence start-up ecosystem, clearing Rs 498 cr proposal, fostering innovation and technology development in support of 300 start-up and 20 partner incubators. Earlier at the Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY) under Electronics Manufacturing Cluster scheme, as a first he approved 500 acre cluster in Andhra Pradesh set up a cost of Rs 340 cr. Other initiatives included, Preferential Market Access Policy and creation of Electronics Development Fund. It is due to his efforts that electronic system design and manufacturing in India under ‘Make in India’ became a reality and has witnessed steady flow of investments.

Going Beyond the Call of Duty

Narendra Nath Veluri

CEO
Agency for New and Renewable Energy Research and Technology (ANERT)

Narendra Nath Veluri

CEO, Agency for New and Renewable Energy Research and Technology (ANERT)

As an Indian Forest Service officer, Narendra Nath Veluri has worked in forest divisions known for human-animal conflict. He brought human deaths to zero during his tenure at Palakkad through out-of-the-box methods adopted that reduced crop damage by elephants to half of that of 2017 damages. He engaged with PVTGs, sensitised them and did solar electrification of all houses. He has implemented Green Income Scheme for 1,000 BPL families by installing a 2kW solar power plant free of cost. A 300 km of solar fencing in Waynad, which was a non-starter for three years, was taken up by Veluri in mission mode and completed in a record time. The Kerala Forest Department has trained the community which owns the project. Under his leadership, all the 600 government buildings in Trivendrum will be solarised by 2023.

Going Beyond the Call of Duty

Divesh Sehara

Secretary
SC and ST Welfare, Bihar

Divesh Sehara

Secretary, SC and ST Welfare, Bihar

Divesh Sehara has a legacy of leaving his footprints behind. In 2010, he led Bihar to become the first state in the country to successfully use Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984 to curb overloading of transport vehicles. As IG Registration, he was instrumental in notifying The Bihar Stamp (Prevention of Under Valuation of Instruments) Amendment Rules, 2013, which significantly enhanced state revenues. If Bihar is proud of the successful dedication and initiation of many infrastructure and road projects, it is due to Divesh’s leadership in implementing Rs 54,000 cr Prime Minister’s Package for Bihar in 2015. The SC & ST Welfare department has benefitted from his vision of reducing pendency for compensation payments to victims. From 8,000 earlier, due to his sustained efforts, it has reduced to double digit in less than a year. Cases are monitored on a daily dashboard and payments are made directly to the beneficiary account.

Going Beyond the Call of Duty

Poonam Malakondaiah

Special Chief Secretary
Andhra Pradesh

Poonam Malakondaiah

Special Chief Secretary, Andhra Pradesh

It is rare to come across an IAS officer firmly rooted in spiritualism. Yes, it does raise many a brow to see a tough, committed, diligently-working-towards-goal kind of government officer. Poonam Malakondaiah is a remarkable officer who has carved a niche for herself through her outstanding work in women's empowerment. She headed a seven-member committee to advise on measures taken for the safety and security of women in the state, which also led to the formation of SHE Teams. She is an ardent advocate of women's literacy for empowerment. She covered more than 10 million farmers under YSR Rythu Bharosa in Phases 1 and 2 with a combined disbursal of over Rs 5,700 cr. Quality inputs were being supplied to farmers through the Rythu Bharosa Kendras and the entire crop insurance premium burden was borne by the government, ensuring hand-holding of farmers from the stage of providing seed to market interventions. She has also encouraged fish culture technologies in large water bodies in the state and set up a Fisheries University.
